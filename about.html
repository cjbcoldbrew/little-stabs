Clothes are maps.

Little Stabs is a sashiko-esque visible mending project for people who like repair, blue cloth, white thread, and objects that feel a little mysterious.

No perfect stitches.

Little Stabs is built around the idea that repair can be beautiful without being precious. The goal is not machine-perfect embroidery. The goal is evidence: a hand, a rhythm, a garment that got to keep going.

Sashiko-esque, respectfully.

The visual language is inspired by blue-and-white sashiko, boro repair, stitched grids, visible mending, and slow textile practice. The brand should teach, credit, and contextualize the tradition while making room for your own beginner-friendly, DIY, fashion-facing expression.

Built for drops.

Start with downloadable pattern sheets, a visible mending guide, and a curated supply page. Add physical goods later: patches, totes, hats, jackets, and one-off embroidered garments.
